       \alpha, \beta 
            &::= \; \ast \; | \; \alpha \rightarrow \beta
            &\text{Types} \\
       M, N &::= V \; \, | \; M \; N \; | \; \lambda(V : \alpha).M

\Ex{Why $x : \alpha$ in the Contexts set, and not 
   $V : \alpha$?}
   {$V$ is the variable set in our \textit{object} language, 
    meaning it is part of the syntax of the language we are
    defining. \\ $x$ represents a variable in our \text{meta}
    language - the language we use to specify and reason about
    our object language.}
}
