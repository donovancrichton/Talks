### Algebraic Structures as Interfaces ###

Why are our interfaces (typeclasses) in functional languages
called things like 'Monoid', 'Functor' and 'Monad'?

Where do these names come from? 

This talk explores the relationship between
familiar interfaces in functional programming, 
and their equivalence in mathematics: the algebraic structure.
We will explore the relationship in the Idris2 programming language.
Specifically we will cover:

Underlying definitions of algebraic structures.,
Monoids, Semigroups, Functors, Monads.,
Free Algebra.,
An Algebraic Introduction to Categories.,
Monoids as Algebra vs Monoids as Categories.,
Overloading of mathematical terms.,

This talk is suitable for intermediate functional programmers who can 
read the ML family of languages (Haskell, SML, etc).
The only mathematics required is to understand that (x, y) is a pair 
of x and y.
